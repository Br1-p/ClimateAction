Science Demostration

In the first experiment, a glass of water and an ice cube are used to demonstrate how ice changes from a solid to a liquid when it is exposed to heat. The participants show the materials, place the ice cube in the water, and observe it melting. They then explain that this process is called melting and that it is a physical change because the substance remains water. In the second experiment, three filters made of soil, leaves, and grass are tested to find out which material cleans dirty water more effectively. Dirty water is poured through each filter, and the results are observed. The soil allows a lot of dirt to pass through, the leaves stop some larger particles, and the grass produces the clearest water. Finally, the results are compared, and it is concluded that filtration is a physical process that removes dirt from water and that natural materials can help clean water in nature.
